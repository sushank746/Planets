03 IN DEPTH

Jupiter's environment is probably not conductive to life
as we know it. The temperature, pressure and
materials that characterize this planet are most likely
too extreme and volatilenfor organism to adapt to.


While planet Jupiter is an unlikely place for living
things to take hold, the same is not true of some of
its many moons. Europa is one of the likeliest places
to find life elsewhere in our solar system. There is
evidence of a vast ocean just beneath its icy crust,
where life could possibly be supported.

04 MOONS

Jupiter has 53 named moons. Scientists have discovered
14 more. But those 14 moons do not have official names.
Scientists now think Jupiter has a total of 67 moons. The
most recent moons were discovered in 2011. The planet's
four largest moons are Ganymede (GAN-i-meed), Callisto
(kuh-LIS-toe), lo (eye-OH), and Europa (yur-O-puh).
